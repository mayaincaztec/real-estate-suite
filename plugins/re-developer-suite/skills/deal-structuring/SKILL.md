# Deal Structuring

1. State buyer profile, target, objectives and constraints.
2. Build two to four feasible options.
3. Obtain specialist inputs for legal feasibility, tax assumptions, financing, governance, market and project execution as relevant.
4. Compare options consistently across feasibility, tax, timing, control, financing, risk allocation, exit and complexity.
5. Treat tax and legal references as issues to verify against current primary sources; do not rely on migrated historical rates.
6. Recommend a preferred option, fallback, prerequisites, approvals and sequencing.

RE-HQ owns the architecture. RE-Legal supplies legal analysis; it does not own the overall structure.
